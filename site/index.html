CombiCone

Certified triage for combinatorial perturbation screens.

Which combinations are worth running? A combinatorial screen explodes super-linearly: 100 single perturbations imply ~5,000 pairs and ~160,000 triples. You cannot measure them all, and most combinations are additive — they do nothing the two singles didn't already do. The decision that costs real money is which combinations produce a cell state the singles cannot reach on their own.

CombiCone answers with certified convex geometry rather than a black box. Every measured single-gene effect is an atom; their non-negative combinations form a cone of everything the singles can additively reach. A combination is emergent when its measured effect lands outside that cone — and the engine returns a model-relative separator certifying the unreachable direction, together with a measurement-noise test so a low-SNR residual is never mistaken for biology.

Scope, stated once and everywhere. "Unreachable" is model-relative: outside the non-negative cone of these measured single-gene effects under this metric — never a claim of biological impossibility. Every result carries this scope field.

Quickstart

pip install -e .            # flat modules: combicone, reachability, screen_ingest, acquisition

# from the command line — ingest, triage, certify, or recommend a screen
combicone triage   screen.h5ad --condition-key perturbation --measured-only
combicone certify  screen.h5ad --combo "SET+CEBPE"
combicone recommend screen.h5ad --batch-size 10 --strategy diversified
import combicone as cc
# rank unmeasured combinations by predicted emergence (training-free)
tr = cc.triage_combinations(singles, single_names, candidate_pairs)
# certify one measured combination against its single-gene cone
cert = cc.certify_emergence(cone_atoms=singles, measured_combo=effect, noise_sd=se)
print(cert.verdict)   # "certified emergent" / "above noise but modest" / "within measurement noise"

Reproduce the paper

One command regenerates the headline emergence certificate from the frozen substrate and verifies it against the committed reference:

bash reproduce_paper.sh          # geometry exact; verdict tiers reproduce; then full test suite

The geometry (cone residual, separator, magnitudes) is deterministic and reproduces exactly; the noise-null (z, p, floor ratio) is a seeded Monte-Carlo quantity that reproduces run-to-run and varies a few percent across bootstrap counts — the verdict tiers the paper's claims rest on are stable.
