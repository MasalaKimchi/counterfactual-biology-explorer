01

The problem

Drug programs fail late, and mostly for the same reason

About nine in ten clinical programmes never reach approval, and the failures cluster where they cost the most. Phase II is the lowest transition in the whole pipeline — roughly 30.7% — and about half of late-stage failures are for lack of efficacy: the target simply was not a way to move the biology. Every AI tool in this space is a forward predictor — it scores what a perturbation would do. None can tell a team, from measured data, that a desired cell-state change is out of reach for the chosen modality. So unreachability is discovered after the screen, or after the trial.

What you’re looking at

The clinical attrition funnel, band width proportional to cumulative survival. Hover a stage to see which of four value levers a feasibility verdict pulls there.

What it means

The leak is concentrated in the middle of the funnel, and human genetic support already carries ~2.6× the approval odds (Minikel 2024). A test that flags unreachable targets before the screen attacks the funnel exactly where it leaks — the motivation for everything below.

02

The reframe

Ask a different question of data you already have

The fix is not a bigger model. A genome-scale Perturb-seq screen already measures an average treatment effect for every gene knockdown. Stack those measured effect vectors and one geometric question falls out: does the target state lie inside the cone of everything the knockdowns can reach? That is a counterfactual query answered by design-based causal inference — using constructs the analysis pipeline already builds.

03

The instrument

A falsifiable verdict on real CRISPRi effects

Here is the oracle on the real 33,983-knockdown × 10,282-gene matrix. For the flagship Th2→Th1 switch in resting CD4⁺ T cells the verdict is partially reachable: knockdown can remove the Th2 program but cannot install the Th1 one. The output is not a similarity score — it is a signed decomposition, a minimal knockdown recipe, and a Farkas/KKT certificate naming the genes no knockdown mix can deliver.

What you’re looking at

Left: the target's exact shadow in the plane of {reachable fit, residual} — the 2-D picture is the true projection of the high-dimensional NNLS fit, not a schematic. Right: pick any of the 12 atlas cells; read its verdict, the signed LOF/GOF/neither split, the greedy recipe, and the activation certificate.

What it means

For the flagship cell: 39% reachable by knockdown, 25% provably needs activation, 35% neither; held-out cosine 0.446 clearing its permutation null, KKT/Farkas residual 1×10⁻¹¹. Master-regulator controls land correctly (GATA3 recovered; TBX21 anti-aligned under knockdown), and across all 12 atlas cells knockdown is never the majority modality — activation and irreducible components always dominate. That last fact is the honest headline: the method is most useful for saying what a knockdown screen cannot do.

04

The trust layer

A verdict is only as good as its assumptions — so test them

A counterfactual claim rests on identifying assumptions. Expert causal inference is making each one explicit, testing the testable ones with data in hand, and quantifying how far a violation must go before the verdict flips. Two views do this: the six-assumption stack, and the instrumental-variable treatment of imperfect knockdown.

What you’re looking at

First panel: the six identifying assumptions, each with its stress-test and a robust / calibrated / caveat grade. Second panel: guide assignment as a randomized instrument and realized knockdown as the treatment — toggle intent-to-treat against the compliance-rescaled (LATE) verdict.

What it means

The headline verdict is robust to the assumptions the design controls: a convex cone is invariant to per-generator rescaling, so the ITT and LATE verdicts coincide to machine precision (|Δcosine| = 2×10⁻¹⁶), and dropping invalid instruments moves it by ≤0.0004. The stack is honest about what it does not yet control — cytokine spillover (SUTVA) is named as the primary external-validity caveat, and the effect-homogeneity test that needs raw single-cell counts is flagged as the single most useful next build.

The payoff

From a verdict to a GO / STOP / REDIRECT shortlist

A feasibility verdict earns its keep when it changes a decision. Take the 102 knockdown nominations the oracle says are actually needed for the flagship axis and cross them against Open Targets tractability and immune-disease genetics.

06

The delta

This capability was missing from fifteen years of methods

Is the feasibility verdict actually new? A survey of 91 prior methods (92 including this work), 2011–2026, places every one on two axes: does it use measured effects, and does it return an achievability verdict?

live · 0 of 91 — the empty quadrant open standalone ↗

What you’re looking at

The capability landscape. 14 methods use measured data; all of them only predict or rank. The measured × achievability quadrant — top-right — is empty.

What it means

Zero of 91 return a feasibility verdict on measured effects. This work is the sole occupant of that quadrant. The novelty is claimed for the method and its decision layer, not for the regulator biology it recovers — recovering GATA3/TBX21 is validation that the instrument works, and the same operator transfers unchanged to a K562 CRISPRa screen (held-out CEBPA at cosine 0.878).
