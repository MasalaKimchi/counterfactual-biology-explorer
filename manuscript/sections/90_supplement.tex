\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS1}
\caption{\textbf{Family-level method $\times$ capability matrix.}
Mean capability over each method family for six capabilities---measured-effect grounding,
feasibility verdict, certificate, infeasibility proof, held-out validation, and combinatorial
support---across the $92$ surveyed methods ($2011$--$2026$). The reachability oracle (top row)
is the only entry that combines full measured-effect grounding with a feasibility verdict,
certificate, and infeasibility proof; every prior family that scores on the verdict/certificate
columns scores near zero on measured-effect grounding, and vice versa.}
\label{fig:supp-matrix}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS2}
\caption{\textbf{Additivity is a bounded approximation across perturbation magnitudes.}
\textbf{(a)}~For the $126$ measured double perturbations in the Norman K562 screen, the cosine
between the measured double and the sum of its two singles (median $0.71$) does not collapse at
large combined perturbation magnitude, but the scatter is real---epistasis exists.
\textbf{(b)}~The relative residual of the additive prediction is bounded and centered below
one, so multi-gene recipes are an extrapolation to be tested at the bench rather than an exact
prediction.}
\label{fig:supp-additivity}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS3}
\caption{\textbf{Positive-control ranking of T-helper regulators.}
Knockdown rank toward the T\textsubscript{H}1 target (log scale; $1$ = most aligned with the
loss-of-function direction, $6871$ = most anti-aligned) for canonical T\textsubscript{H}1 and
T\textsubscript{H}2 regulators. T\textsubscript{H}2 drivers, which must be removed, rank high
(GATA3 at $155/6871$); T\textsubscript{H}1 drivers, which must be raised, rank low (TBX21 at
$6775/6871$). The transcription-factor-versus-marker split is an exploratory, post-hoc
annotation, not a pre-registered test.}
\label{fig:supp-poscontrol}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS4}
\caption{\textbf{Triage funnel and the three actions a verdict enables.}
\textbf{(a)}~The union of top-10 greedy LOF panels contains $102$ unique candidates: $57$
carry a druggable handle and $10$ have a clinical-grade drug, while $45/102$ ($44\%$) are
hard-to-drug.
\textbf{(b)}~Each state-level verdict maps to one action---GO (test a greedy sparse CRISPRi
panel), REDIRECT (test the sign-flipped GOF hypothesis in a CRISPRa/de-repression arm), or STOP
(no signal above the null; seek a different dictionary or modality). Candidate-level
tractability annotations prioritize within the chosen arm. Every call is a falsifiable wet-lab
hypothesis; the instrument triages, it does not validate.}
\label{fig:supp-triage}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS5}
\caption{\textbf{The verdict strengthens under DEG-magnitude weighting.}
For each target, the reachable cosine \textbf{(left)} and the held-out-gene $z$ \textbf{(right)}
under the default uniform metric (open markers) and under a DEG-magnitude weighting
$w_g=\lvert d_g\rvert$ that concentrates the metric on the genes the target actually moves
(filled markers). Reweighting \emph{raises} the reachable cosine for every target---the
Tier-2 T\textsubscript{H}2$\rightarrow$T\textsubscript{H}1 shift rises from $0.627$ to $0.803$
at rest---and the held-out $z$ stays far above the $z=3$ significance floor, so the signal is
not diluted by unchanged genes. This addresses the signal-dilution critique of
\citet{Mejia2026}; the unweighted metric remains the default and reproduces the published
numbers exactly.}
\label{fig:supp-deg}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS6}
\caption{\textbf{Each verdict is calibrated between its shuffled floor and an
interpolated-duplicate ceiling.}
\textbf{(left)}~For each target, the observed reachable cosine (unweighted, circle;
DEG-weighted, diamond) placed between a shuffled-gene floor and an interpolated-duplicate
ceiling (whiskers). Both floor and ceiling rise under weighting, so the observed value's
placement between them is stable. \textbf{(right)}~The scale-free dynamic-range fraction---the
observed cosine expressed as a position between floor and ceiling---barely moves under weighting
(Tier-2 rest $0.447\rightarrow0.499$), confirming that the strengthening in
Fig.~\ref{fig:supp-deg} is a genuine, calibrated signal rather than a scaling artifact. The
dynamic-range calibration is computed on the rest condition (Monte-Carlo cost).}
\label{fig:supp-calib}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS7}
\caption{\textbf{Cross-cell-type transfer: direction transfers, the greedy recipe does not.}
The unchanged operator applied to the \citet{Replogle2022} K562 and RPE1 essential-gene screens
($843$ shared perturbations, $2{,}832$ shared genes). \textbf{(a)}~A single perturbation's
effect \emph{direction} transfers moderately (matched cross-type cosine median $0.35$; grey
band, shuffled-gene null $\pm95\%$). \textbf{(b)}~The reachability \emph{verdict} transfers only
as a graded residual: the median residual norm is systematically worse under a cross-type basis
(hatched) than a within-type basis, for both K562 and RPE1 targets. \textbf{(c)}~The greedy
\emph{recipe} does not transfer---same-target Jaccard overlap ($0.11$) collapses toward the
random-selection null ($0.05$) under a cross-type basis. Direction is portable across cell
types; the specific recipe is not.}
\label{fig:supp-transfer}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figS8}
\caption{\textbf{The activation certificate is reproducible under a gene-axis split.}
\textbf{(a)}~Mean rank of each top certificate gene when the certificate is refit on a random
half of the gene axis (vertical bars, spread over $12$ splits), against its published rank on
the full cone fit (colour, fraction of half-fits keeping the gene in the top $30$). The
highest-demand genes (LYAR, CRTAM, GBP5, IRF8) hug the identity line; the tail (E2F3, LUZP1,
SNTB2) drifts. \textbf{(b)}~Rank agreement across independent halves (cross-half Spearman
$\rho\approx0.65$) and between a half-fit and the full fit ($\rho\approx0.84$) is stable across
$12$ random split seeds and far above the shuffle null (grey band; cross-half $z\approx35$).}
\label{fig:supp-split}
\end{figure}

biological reading.}
\textbf{(a)}~The top $20$ certificate genes ranked by unmet upward demand, coloured by
literature direction-of-effect (SUPPORTS $4$, PLAUSIBLE $7$, UNKNOWN $9$). The highest-demand

validated list of activation targets; for the negative regulators, a useful intervention may be
opposite in sign to naive activation. This cross-reference is exploratory and post-hoc.}
